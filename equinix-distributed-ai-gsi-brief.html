Overview

This brief provides an internal go-to-market plan for aligning Equinix Distributed AI capabilities with Global Systems Integrators (GSIs) such as TCS, HCLTech, Wipro, and Infosys. It is designed to provide a concise, partner-specific view that will help Equinix solution architects and partner teams navigate joint AI opportunities, structure co-selling motions, and support field engagements with clear talking points and a mutual value narrative.

Platform Narrative

Equinix Distributed AI – Differentiators for GSI Partnerships

Equinix’s global platform offers unique value to GSIs looking to deliver AI, analytics, and digital transformation programs for their enterprise clients. While hyperscale clouds provide compute and managed AI services, Equinix provides the distributed, interconnected infrastructure that brings AI closer to data, users, and ecosystems, enabling GSIs to accelerate enterprise AI deployments. Key talking points include:

Vendor-Neutral Ecosystem & Interconnection: Equinix is a neutral platform where enterprises can securely connect to multiple clouds, networks, SaaS providers, and AI ecosystems. This enables GSIs to design architectures that integrate multiple AI services (e.g. different cloud AI stacks, specialized ML tools, data marketplaces) while providing on-demand, high-bandwidth connectivity across providers.

Global Reach with Local Compliance: With 270+ data centers in 70+ metros across 6 continents, Equinix allows GSIs to host AI workloads close to users and data while meeting local data residency and regulatory requirements. This makes it easier to satisfy compliance (critical in BFSI and healthcare) without sacrificing scale.

Edge-to-Cloud Infrastructure & Performance: Equinix is building a Distributed AI platform, enabling GPU-ready colocation, high-density power, interconnection to cloud AI services, and support for edge inference nodes. GSIs can deploy AI training infrastructure in regional hubs and inference workloads closer to end-users, reducing latency and improving real-time decision-making. This extends public cloud AI into a more distributed pattern – from training to inference – with reliable performance globally.

Data Privacy, Security & Trust: Equinix’s interconnection model avoids routing sensitive data over the public internet. GSIs can leverage private connectivity and secure data exchange patterns to move or access data between enterprise locations, clouds, and partners, reducing exposure and improving control. Combined with Equinix’s physical and logical security, this creates a strong foundation for trusted AI deployments.

Simplified Deployment & Co-Innovation: Equinix handles the underlying infrastructure complexity – power, cooling (including liquid cooling readiness), space, and interconnection. GSIs can focus on delivering AI platforms, applications, and managed services, while relying on Equinix as a consistent global foundation. Equinix can also support joint labs and innovation environments where GSIs and clients test AI architectures and solutions on demand, accelerating deployment timelines for joint solutions.

With these differentiators in mind, the following sections outline GSI-specific positioning, value alignment, and talking points to help Equinix teams pitch Distributed AI to maximize joint go-to-market success.
